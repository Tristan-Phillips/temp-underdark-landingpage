Privacy & encryption

Content is encrypted before it leaves your device
Cards, lorebooks, personas, and images are encrypted in your browser using AES-256-GCM. The key is derived from your password, which never leaves your device. The server stores ciphertext. The operator cannot read your stored content.
No account. No email. No recovery.
Access is a generated code, not an identity, not an email address. There is no password reset, no way to recover a lost account. Lose the code, lose the account. That is the design, not an oversight.
What gets logged
When AI generates something, the server records: when, which model, how many tokens, which instance. The prompt and the response are never written down. This is wired into the architecture, not a setting that can be toggled.
What end-to-end encryption does not cover: when you send a prompt, it travels to NanoGPT's servers in plaintext to produce a response. It is protected by TLS in transit, but NanoGPT processes it — their privacy policy applies to that request. Once the response returns, it is encrypted before storage. This is inherent to how AI generation works. It is written here, not in a footnote.
